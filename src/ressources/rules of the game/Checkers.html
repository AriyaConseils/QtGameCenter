Rules of Checkers

Checkers is a classic board game played between two players. Here are the rules:

  • The game is played on a 8x8 board with alternating light and dark squares.
  • Each player starts with 12 pieces, placed on the dark squares of the three rows closest to them.
  • Players take turns moving one piece diagonally forward to an adjacent empty square.
  • If a player's piece jumps over an opponent's piece to land on an empty square, the opponent's piece is captured and removed from the board.
  • If a piece reaches the opponent's back row, it becomes a King and gains the ability to move both forward and backward.
  • The game is won when a player captures all the opponent's pieces or blocks all their possible moves.

Enjoy the game and may the best strategist win!
