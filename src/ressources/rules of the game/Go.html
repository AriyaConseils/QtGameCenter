Rules of Go

Go is an ancient strategy board game for two players. Here are the basic rules:

  • The game is played on a square board of 19x19 lines, but smaller boards (like 9x9 or 13x13) are also used for beginners or quick games.
  • Each player has an unlimited supply of pieces called stones. One player uses black stones, and the other uses white stones.
  • Players take turns placing one stone on the intersections of the grid lines.
  • The goal is to surround more territory on the board than your opponent.
  • Stones are removed from the board when they are completely surrounded by the opponent's stones. This is called a capture.
  • The game ends when both players agree there are no more beneficial moves, and the score is calculated based on controlled territory and captured stones.
  • The player with the highest score wins.

Enjoy the game of Go, where every move is a step towards mastering strategy!
